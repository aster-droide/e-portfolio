Numerical Analysis

This module introduces key aspects of mathematics and statistics for data science and artificial intelligence. Aside from a first introduction in R, it is expected to gain a solid understanding of the NumPy library in Python.

Each unit below contains a short description of the learning objective, along with its respective Data Activity if present.

Notes & reflections

Unit 1

Getting myself familiar with the material for this module. A first introduction into R, which seems fairly easy to pick up on coming from Python.

  • Data Activity notes & findings

Unit 2

Continuing the journey through R, this unit also introduces some key mathematical concepts such as vectors and matrices.

  • Data Activity notes & findings

Unit 3

Most of unit 3 was spent on recapping the theory on mathematical vectors. Scalar multiplication, vector addition and subtraction, and vector components from magnitude and direction. I've put this learning into active practice with the Khan Academy Precalculus course sections of Composite and inverse functions and Vectors. I further went back to cover the complete NumPy basics with the help of this NumPy Crash Course.

  • Data Activity notes & findings

Unit 4

This unit entailed a fair bit of reading and studying of the following topics:

  • Probability topics such as independent and mutually exclusive events along with rules of probability and ways to present probability statistics visually.
  • Discrete Random Variables of hypergeometric, binomial, geometric, and poisson distributions while experimenting with their respective functions.
  • Continuous Random Variables with the uniform distribution and the exponential distribution and their respective probability density function for the curve and cumulative distribution functions for the area under the curve.
  • The Normal Distribution and how to use the Standard Normal Distribution with Z-Scores.
  • Sampling methods such as bootstrap, stratified, and random sampling.
  • Matrices section from the Kahn Academy Precalculus course. Addition, subtraction, scalar multiplication, dot product multiplication, identity matrices, and finally inverse matrices.
I further worked through the unit's probability activities and exercises of the reading material to ensure comprehension. This unit did not include a Data Activity.

Unit 5

Besides a Data Activity, this week kicked off the start of the Collaborative Discussion 1. It was a fun and challenging exercise of which further reflection is detailed in the section below.

  • Data Activity notes & findings
  • Collaborative Discussion 1 - Initial Post

Unit 6

I found unit 6 a bit heavy. With the statistics test approaching in unit 7 I was aware that I had to tie up all loose learning ends. The reading list for this unit was long and although interesting, a bit hard to get through. I continued with the “Additional Reading” by following the recommended Khan Academy courses. I worked through the significance testing (hypothesis testing course), and the Confidence Intervals course and further explored any topics that I felt were necessary in order to gain complete comprehension. I am finding the content from Khan academy extremely useful and well explained, it truly captures the concepts that I need to know for this module well.

The “Statistics Quiz” that is part of the formative activities for this unit was not very useful. It was basic and limited with ambiguous wording.

In this week the discussion from unit 5 continues, we are meant to comment on our peers' presentations. Sadly only one other student participated in this exercise, but we have had a good back and forth on the topic and I feel that I have definitely gained a lot from it overall.

Unit 7

In preparation for the exam I further worked through Khan's course on summary statistics along with various other online sources. At this stage, I felt like I had a full understanding of all the concepts that had come up thus far.

The exam itself was harder than I expected - but solely due to the time constraint. I had luckily independently studied all topics much deeper than what was deemed required during the seminars, but I did not have enough time to answer the questions properly. I had to rush through it and made unnecessary mistakes because of this. I submitted the exam within time but immediately noticed silly errors. If any consolation, I do know that I am proficient in these topics and that is what matters at the end of the day. I hope I will get a good grade.
