Unit 7

COLLABORATIVE DISCUSSION - SUMMARY POST

This has been an excellent educational exercise that has guided me into the first steps of data visualisation with R.

As mentioned in my Initial Post, I spent a decent amount of time on getting the data to look the way I wanted it to look. I enjoyed the fact that Feaviour (2023a) independently took a similar approach.

Feaviour (2023b) suggested putting the “no opinion” variable in the centre of the graph - to which I agree that it makes more sense.

Sadly no other students participated in this discussion, however Leigh and I had a little back and forth with tips and reflections for each other. When Feaviour (2023b) asked me how I treated my data to look like it did, I was of course more than happy to share them. In fact, it would have probably made sense to add these into the reflection in my Initial Post.

In addition to the full-data graphic visualisation that both Feaviour and I presented, Feaviour (2023a) also presented the data split up into different themed tables. I think the chosen table graphics would certainly look well in a paper, and it gives space to talk a bit more in depth about each individual theme.

In short, I enjoyed the exercise and I feel like I gained a lot from it - both through figuring out how to present data and make inferences from it as well as from the way Leigh has approached the task.
